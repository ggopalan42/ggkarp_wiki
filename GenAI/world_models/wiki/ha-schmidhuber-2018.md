# World Models (Ha & Schmidhuber, 2018)

The canonical modern paper and the clearest single on-ramp to the field. arXiv:1803.10122 (v4, May 2018). Interactive companion: https://worldmodels.github.io

**Thesis:** Split an RL agent into a large **world model** (trained unsupervised to compress and predict the environment) and a small **controller** (trained to act using the world model's features). Because most capacity lives in the world model, the controller can be tiny and trained with simple black-box optimization. The agent can even be trained *entirely inside its own hallucinated dream* generated by the world model, then transferred back to the real environment.

## Architecture: V + M + C

Three components inspired by the human cognitive loop:

- **V (Vision) — Convolutional VAE.** Compresses each input image frame (resized to 64x64x3) into a small latent vector `z` (Nz = 32 for CarRacing, 64 for Doom). Trained to reconstruct frames (L2 reconstruction loss + KL loss). ~4.3-4.4M parameters.
- **M (Memory) — MDN-RNN.** An LSTM with a Mixture Density Network output layer. Models the distribution of the next latent, `P(z_{t+1} | a_t, z_t, h_t)`, as a mixture of Gaussians (5 mixtures). A **temperature `tau`** controls sampling uncertainty. For Doom it also predicts the `done` flag. ~422K (CarRacing) to ~1.7M (Doom) parameters.
- **C (Controller) — linear model.** `a_t = W_c [z_t h_t] + b_c`. Deliberately tiny (867 params for CarRacing, 1088 for Doom) so almost all complexity sits in V and M. Trained separately via **CMA-ES** (evolution strategy), which suits its few-thousand-parameter search space and only needs the final cumulative reward.

The loop: V encodes the observation to `z_t`; C acts on `[z_t, h_t]`; M updates its hidden state `h_{t+1}` from `(z_t, a_t)`. See the four axes in [[four-axis-reading-framework]].

## Experiments and key results

- **CarRacing-v0 (continuous control from pixels).** First known method to "solve" it. Full world model (z + h) scored **906 ± 21** over 100 trials vs. the 900 threshold; using only `z` (no memory `h`) gave wobbly driving (632 ± 251), showing the predictive hidden state `h` is what enables stable, anticipatory control. V and M trained from 10,000 random rollouts; no reward signal reaches V/M (only C sees reward).
- **VizDoom: Take Cover (learning inside a dream).** M is turned into a full simulated environment (predicts next `z` and `done`). The agent is trained **entirely inside the M-generated dream** in latent space (no rendering, no game engine), then transferred to the real game: ~**1100** time steps vs. the 750 solve threshold.

## Key concepts and findings

- **Dream / hallucinated training.** Once M predicts observations, rewards, and termination, it can replace the real environment as an RL world. Training in latent space is far cheaper than running the real engine.
- **Cheating the world model.** The controller can find **adversarial policies** that exploit M's imperfections (e.g. making monsters never fire) that fail in reality. This is the core weakness of learning a policy inside a learned model.
- **Temperature `tau` as a regularizer.** Increasing `tau` injects uncertainty into the dream, making it harder to exploit M's flaws and improving transfer. Best Doom transfer at `tau = 1.15` (1092 ± 556); too low (`tau = 0.1`) causes mode collapse (no fireballs) and policies that fail in reality; too high makes the dream unlearnable. A tunable realism/exploitability tradeoff.
- **Iterative training.** For harder environments, alternate between collecting new rollouts and retraining M (predicting `x, r, a, done`) and C, so the world model improves on regions the agent newly reaches. M's prediction loss can double as a **curiosity** signal.
- **Lineage.** Distills RNN world-model + controller ideas from Schmidhuber 1990-2015 ("Making the World Differentiable", "Learning to Think"). Uses evolution for C (simplicity, generality) rather than RL through RNNs.

## Framework placement

See [[four-axis-reading-framework]]: reconstruction-based representation (VAE pixels), RNN/MDN dynamics, used for both planning-free reactive control and policy learning in imagination, supervised by reconstruction (V) plus reward via the controller (C).

## Limitations noted by the authors

- VAE trained standalone may encode task-irrelevant detail (it is unsupervised, so it cannot know what matters).
- LSTM-based M has limited capacity and suffers catastrophic forgetting; future work suggests higher-capacity models or external memory.
- Step-by-step simulation only — no human-like hierarchical planning (the more general "Learning to Think" framing allows C to reuse M's subroutines).
